Proposed Projects

It is clear that Sierra Leone has a lot of potential, but many things need to be done to support the country and allow citizens to focus on creating jobs and producing and exporting goods. The first project proposal is to expand and increase the density of the nation's mobile cellular network. Bringing the mobile cellular networks to the smaller towns and rural areas of Sierra Leone will connect the remaining 17% of the population that is not currently subscribed to a mobile cellular plan. Connecting the entire nation would be an enormous accomplishment. Increasing the density and redundancy of the cellular networks would ensure that subscribers in highly populated areas will always have service and the ability to access the network with their device.

While creating new mobile cellular towers and expanding the current network, the new Africa Coast to Europe (ACE) submarine fiber optic cable connection in Freetown will be used to upgrade current and new cellular infrastructure. With a fiber optic connection to each cellular tower, mobile data services can be provided to subscribers for a very low cost. Current cellular subscribers in the country to not have many mobile data options.

The extremely high mobile cellular subscription rate is one of Sierra Leone's biggest advantages. With the vast majority of the population connected to each other and the world via their mobile device, applications and services can begin to be created to help support the nation. The second proposal is to create a service that allows citizens to receive needed medical information and report unknown illnesses. Having up-to-date and specific medical information at their fingertips will allow the people to stop major disease outbreaks before they even start. The ability to aggregate illness and location information from citizens will allow authorities to watch and track the spread of illnesses and step in to intervene before diseases spread too far. These tools could also allow citizens to report safety and infrastructure needs.

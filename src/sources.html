Sources

  1. ACE : Début de construction de la liaison entre Sao Tomé &Principe et Cape Town. (n.d.). Retrieved April 21, 2016, from https://www.ace-submarinecable.com/

    The Africa Coast to Europe website is the organization website for the creator or the ACE submarine fiber optic cable. This source is used as it is direct information about the cable from the cable's creator. The cable is the first submarine cable to connect Sierra Leone to the vast international broadband network.

  2. The World Factbook: Sierra Leone. (2016). Retrieved April 21, 2016, from https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/sl.html

    The CIA world factbook is a collection of very detailed facts and statistics about nations and organizations all around the world. This information is managed by the United States government.

  3. Kabbah, H. (n.d.). Sierra Leone Legal System and Legal Research - GlobaLex. Retrieved April 21, 2016, from http://www.nyulawglobal.org/globalex/Sierra_Leone.html

    This webpage was written by a professor and researcher at NYU with in depth knowledge of the Sierra Leone governmental system. She has studied at multiple Sierra Leonean universities and has received her Sierra Leone bar in 1999.

  4. Elections in Sierra Leone. (n.d.). Retrieved April 21, 2016, from http://africanelections.tripod.com/sl.html

    This website is a collection of facts and statistics about elections in most African nations. Information is aggregated directly from the source of each country's election offices and officials.

  5. Ebola Virus Disease in West Africa — The First 9 Months of the Epidemic and Forward Projections. (2014). New England Journal of Medicine N Engl J Med,371(16), 1481-1495. doi:10.1056/nejmoa1411100

  6. Pham, J. (2005). Child soldiers, adult interests: The global dimensions of the Sierra Leonean tragedy. New York: Nova Science.

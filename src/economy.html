Economy

In the past few years Sierra Leone has seen a major growth in GDP and exports. Sierra Leone's purchasing power parity gross domestic product (ppp GDP) for 2014 was $12.92 billion. However, in the later months of 2014 and the beginning months of 2015 brought the Ebola virus to Western Africa. Sierra Leone took the brunt force of this widespread disease and the economy of the nation felt the pain. The industrial production growth rate for 2015 was -51% compared to 2014. The ppp GDP also dipped to $9.833 billion compared to 2014's $12.92 billion. With the slowing down of the Ebola virus and only mild flare-ups in the nation, the economy is back on track to expand and grow as it begins to define its place in the global economy. Industries and exports of Sierra Leone include diamond mining, iron ore, rutile, bauxite mining, small-scale manufacturing, petroleum refining, small commercial ship repair. [2] [5]

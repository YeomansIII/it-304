A Pressing Need

Sierra Leone has a very large mobile cellular subscriber rate, but service is spotty and devices can not always be used as intended. Had the services been available during the Ebola outbreak, cases could have been more easily tracked and managed to limit the spread. As a developing nation, widespread outbreaks of deadly diseases are much more likely. Technologies and services need to be able to help the nation's population stop the spread of the disease. [ 5]

The nation needs a massive expansion and upgrade of its mobile cellular network to connect the remaining 17% of the population that does not currently have a subscription, as well as provide a more robust service to current subscribers so that they can use their devices as intended. Alongside this, new services and tools need to be made available to the public that take advantage of the high mobile cellular subscription rate to monitor and track the outbreak of diseases and illnesses that pose a threat to the entire nation, as was witnessed with the Ebola epidemic.

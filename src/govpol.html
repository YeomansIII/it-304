Government and Politics

Sierra Leone is a constitutional republic with a legislative, executive, and judiciary branches. The legislature is unicameral, meaning that there is only one chamber, compared to the United States system of two chambers (the House and the Senate). The president, the lead of the executive branch, is directly elected by the people. The judiciary is headed by the Chief Justice. [3]

The current president of Sierra Leone is Ernest Bai Koroma, who assumed office on the 17th of September, 2007. Before being elected president, Koroma served as the Leader of the All People's Congress (2002-present), the Minority Leader of Parliament (2005-2007), and a Member of Parliament from the Bombali District (2002-2007). [4]

The only three parties in Sierra Leone that hold seats in parliament are the All People's Congress (APC), the Sierra Leone People's Party (SLPP), and the People's Movement for Democratic Change (PMDC). The APC and SLPP are the two oldest parties in Sierra Leone, dating back to 1962 and 1951, respectively. The APC currently holds more seats in parliament as well as holds the current presidency. [4]

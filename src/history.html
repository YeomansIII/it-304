History

Sierra Leone has been inhabited continuously for over 2,500 years. The inhabiting populations migrated from other portions of Africa. The dense tropical rainforests of Sierra Leone has protected the inhabitants all through history as a natural barrier to invasions and ideas like Islam.

Sierra Leoneon troops marching past then British Govornor of Sierra Leone

After the American Revolutionary War, the British Empire created a settlement around the now Freetown in Sierra Leone. The Empire created this settlement to resettle thousands of African Americans that were freed during the war, as well as the poorer blacks of London. The African American settlers built the settlement, as they did in the American South, and continued many American traditions. Being on the west coast of Africa, the settlers were subject to the illegal slave trade and constantly feared being re-enslaved. The Empire lacked in providing essential materials and protection to the settlement. [6]

Sierra Leone gained independence from the British empire on April 27th, 1961. The government that was established after independence only lasted 6 years, and what followed was 24 years of military coups and a one-party state until the breakout of the Sierra Leone civil war in 1991. The civil war did not end until 2002, severely depleting natural resources and destroying significant amounts of infrastructure. The civil war ended after British troops sent to the country to remove foreign nationals clashed with the rebels and ended up fully defeating them and restoring order. Many elements of the British Army remain in the country today helping restore infrastructure and providing financial and medical assistance. [6]

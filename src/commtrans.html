Communication and Transportation

Sierra Leone has a growing communications and transportation infrastructure. The vast majority of communications takes place over mobile cellular networks, with 4.8 million mobile subscribers (83% of the population), while there are only 16,500 fixed line subscriptions (<1% of the population). Mobile cellular coverage is limited to few highly concentrated cities including Freetown and Kenema. Radio broadcast is the main mean of public media with only less than 5 public television stations. The country contains only 282 internet hosts and 85,600 internet users (1.5% of the population). [2]

Sierra Leone has an extensive road network, but the vast majority of these roads are unpaved. Unpaved roads limit the transportation of goods and services throughout the country. There are approximately 11,300 km of roads, where only 904 km of those roads are actually paved. The country manages 8 airports, with only one paved runway at Lungi International Airport outside of Freetown. The major ports in the nation are Freetown, Pepel, and Sherbro Islands. [2]

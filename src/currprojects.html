Current Development Projects

ACE Cable landing in 2011
Sierra Leone's infrastructure has been expanding rapidly since the end of the nation's painful civil war. One of the most prominent infrastructure developments was the connection of Freetown to the Africa Coast to Europe (ACE) submarine fiber optic cable [1]. This 17,000 km submarine fiber optic cable now connects Sierra Leone to 16 other West African nations as well as Portugal and France [1]. This is the first submarine fiber optic cable that has been landed in Sierra Leone. This connection allows Sierra Leone to connect to international broadband at a lower cost as well as provide significantly higher speeds. Providing affordable and fast broadband internet allows increased social and economic development [1].
